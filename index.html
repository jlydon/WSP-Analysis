Site Intent

Project Analysis Overview

The homepage for Primrose Center, Inc leaves much to be desired. The goal is to make the site more welcoming. This can easily be done by correcting some validation errors, arranging content in a meaningful way, and improving the overall user experiance by arranging navigation elements in a less redundant way.

W3C Validation Errors

Primrose Center fails the W3C validation with 26 markup errors and 0 CSS errors. 26 Markup errors is just poor practice and this site only passes a CSS validation because it has little style to validate. These errors need to be addressed and some creativity added to the style.

Content & Presentation

All of Primrose Center's markup is quite messy and does not take advantage of newer CSS3 techniques. This problem can be addressed simply by isolating the actual content of the site and remove anything that is not symantically important.

Page Layout

The site displays no sign of proximity as it has both an oversized navigation on the left side, and a poorly styled navigation bar located under the call to action. The logo is far too big as it distracts the user from the content. Lackluster margins and padding make readability difficult. Company information is not promenently displayed. My solution to these problems:

  • Re-arrange the logo, resize it to be less intrusive
  • Horizontal menu at the top, underneath the logo
  • Remove aside navigation, it's redundant

User Interface

Currently, reading Primrose Center's website is difficult on the eyes. Overly used gradients, limited colors, and a font library not relevent to the topic it represents. We will create a better user experiance by choosing a font type and color scheme that contrasts with the mood of the site. Organization of the call to action will be used to extract important data out of the site for the users easy accessablity. Backdoor needs to be larger and more dominantly posted.

Competitor Comparison

Primrose Center is a non-profit organization, thus "competition" is more about site hits than anything else. When you enter the term "people with disabilities" the following sites appear for having the highest Alexa Traffic Rank:

  • Indiana University (Rank 6,505)
  • Minnesota State (Rank 11,001)
  • State of Illinois (Rank 12,677)

Iu.edu has a three-month global Alexa traffic rank of 6,505. Visitors to the site view an average of 4.3 unique pages per day. Visitors to the site spend roughly four minutes per visit to the site and 35 seconds per pageview. The site is particularly popular among users in the city of Indianapolis (where it is ranked #41). Iu.edu is located in the US

State.il.us's three-month global Alexa traffic rank is 12,677. The site has been online for more than nine years. The site's visitors view an average of 4.6 unique pages per day. Compared with all internet users, State.il.us's users are disproportionately Caucasian, and they are disproportionately moderately educated, higher-income women between the ages of 35 and 65 who browse from home. The site is located in the US.

State.mn.us is ranked #11,001 in the world according to the three-month Alexa traffic rankings, and the site has been online for at least nine years. The time spent in a typical visit to the site is about four minutes, with 37 seconds spent on each pageview. Compared with internet averages, State.mn.us's audience tends to be Caucasian; it also appeals more to moderately educated women over the age of 45 who have incomes over $30,000 and have more children. About 85% of visitors to the site come from the US, where it has attained a traffic rank of 2,238.
